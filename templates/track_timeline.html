Design Notes

1
Deck Status Bar

Shows which tracks are currently loaded on each deck (LIVE / CUE). Deck A (LIVE) is read-only — you can't edit its phases mid-set. Deck B (CUE) is the natural edit target before the mix transition.

2
Playlist Browser

All tracks in the DJ's library with a mini color map strip showing the auto-detected phases at a glance. Tap any track to expand its phase editor. LIVE and CUE badges mark which tracks are on the decks.

3
Phase Editor

Auto-detected sections from audio analysis — energy, frequency, and BPM transitions. Each phase shows its color, mode, and energy level. Tap Edit › on any row to change color, mode, or energy. Highlighted phases (Drop, Chorus) show with a tinted background.

4
Syncs to Live

Saving changes here updates the color map and mode triggers that fire automatically on the Live screen. No waveform scrubbing here — that view lives in Live where it's useful live.

5
Phase Labels ⚡

Drops and other key moments are flagged with ⚡ and a highlight glow. These are the moments that trigger the highlight color burst automatically during the set. DJ can rename, remove, or reassign them.
